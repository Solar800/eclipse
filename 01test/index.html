Digital Citizenship

What is Digital Citizenship?

Digital citizenship is the ability to use technology and the internet safely, responsibly, and respectfully. It involves understanding the norms of appropriate behavior in digital spaces, similar to how one behaves as a citizen in a physical community.

What id Digital Law?

Digital law, often referred to as cyber law, encompasses the legal rights, regulations, and ethical standards that govern behavior in the digital world. In the context of digital citizenship, it establishes the "electronic responsibility" for actions and deeds performed online.

What is AUP?

An Acceptable Use Policy (AUP) is a formal set of rules and guidelines that define how an organization's computer networks, websites, and technology resources may and may not be used. It acts as a digital "code of conduct" and is a core component of digital citizenship and law

What is Plagirism?

Plagiarism is the act of presenting someone else's work, ideas, or intellectual property as your own without proper attribution or permission.

Often described as a form of "intellectual fraud" or "literary theft," it is a major violation of digital citizenship and academic integrity

What is Acidemic Dishonesty?

Academic dishonesty refers to any form of cheating or deception that occurs in a formal academic exercise to gain an unfair advantage.

It is a violation of academic integrity and is treated as a serious offense by educational institutions because it undermines the value of education and the authenticity of student work.
